Mission Statement:

Be at ease and chat openly. "EASE" is a web app created by four teenage girls who wanted to create something to combat social issues, such as mental health. Aligned with the sentiment that "a problem half shared is a problem half solved" our app encourages communications between users in hopes of creating a safe space for sharing issues, feelings, and building companionship. Here at EASE we have created a web app that is designed to give users a safe and comfortable environment, where they can converse openly with whomever they choose. By creating this app we anticipated that issues faced by people in their day to day lives can be assuaged through open discussion and companionship.

About Us:

In the summer of 2018, we five girls met at “Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program.” SIP is a 7-week program, during which 20 girls who of which are either rising juniors or seniors in high school are able to learn about computer science through real-world projects in art and storytelling, robotics, video games, websites, and apps. Throughout this process as our knowledge of computer science strengthen so did our bond with each other. We created a support network of girls. We were able to lean on and learn from each other and it’s very encouraging to see a group of girls openly express their love for computer science and actively pursue a future in it through the completion of this program. It lets us know that whereas computer science was a male dominated field the future for women in the computer science field is not bleak .
